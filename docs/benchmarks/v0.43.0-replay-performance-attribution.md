## Methodology

The benchmark uses two timing tracks:

- Production benchmark: direct `HistoricalReplayService.run()` wall-clock timing, with no wrappers, patches, monkeypatching, or copied replay loop. This is the authoritative production timing.
- Instrumented attribution benchmark: still calls the real `HistoricalReplayService.run()`, but wraps the replay module bindings and injected services/models with call-through timing proxies.

The harness does not copy the replay loop. The only replay production-code change is a private helper, `_copy_replay_prefix()`, that preserves the exact previous expression `prices.iloc[: position + 1].copy(deep=True)` so prefix copy has a real timing boundary.

DataFrame construction is completed before timing. For comparability across 100/300/500 bars, the harness generates one deterministic max-bars frame for the requested benchmark set and each scenario receives a deep-copied prefix of that same frame. With the same seed, the 500-bar frame's first 300 bars exactly equal the 300-bar scenario frame, and its first 100 bars exactly equal the 100-bar scenario frame.

Each scenario uses one warm-up run and three measured production runs, followed by one warm-up and three measured instrumented runs. The harness calls `gc.collect()` before every warm-up and measured timing for both tracks and for each serialization item. GC is not permanently disabled and samples are not filtered.

Serialization is measured only after `HistoricalReplayService.run()` returns. The benchmark measures `HistoricalReplayResult.to_dict()`, `json.dumps(prebuilt_result_dict)`, `summarize_historical_replay(result)`, `summary.to_dict()`, and `json.dumps(prebuilt_summary_dict)` as separate items.

Measured scenarios use deterministic synthetic daily OHLCV data, one symbol, one provider, positive OHLC values, `high >= low`, non-negative volume, and two baseline strategies: `baseline_trend_regime@1.0.0` and `baseline_volatility_regime@1.0.0`.

## Segment Timing and Call Counts

Each segment reports median cumulative time across the three instrumented runs. `prod share` divides the instrumented segment median by the uninstrumented production median. `instr share` divides it by the instrumented median.

Segment overlap rules: wrappers are call-through and non-nested for the measured replay stages. Structure analysis and observation construction are not nested; state evaluation and strategy evaluation are not nested; no object is wrapped by two timing proxies for the same segment. Segment totals still include wrapper overhead. Because production totals and segment totals come from different timing tracks and different samples, production shares are normalized attribution ratios and are not guaranteed to sum to exactly 100%. Residual means `instrumented total - measured segment totals` and should be read only as orchestration/model/unattributed residual.

## v0.42.1 Comparability

The v0.42.1 handoff recorded local medians for two default strategies: 100 bars `0.924s`, 300 bars `3.740s`, and 500 bars `7.304s`. This review searched the repository for retained benchmark scripts, commands, data generation code, and references to those numbers. Only the handoff numbers were found; the script and synthetic data used for v0.42.1 were not retained.

Current v0.43.0 medians are 100 bars `1.071s`, 300 bars `6.573s`, and 500 bars `18.908s`. These are not directly comparable to the v0.42.1 numbers because the workload cannot be proven identical. The most likely explanation for the difference is workload shape: the current deterministic series is intentionally oscillatory and structure-heavy, while the prior ad hoc benchmark may have used simpler monotonic or near-monotonic data that produced fewer pivots, candidate levels, zones, and touch observations. This report does not classify the difference as a production regression.

## Complexity Trend

Measured facts:

- Production median grew from 1.07s at 100 bars to 18.91s at 500 bars.
- Structure analysis median per call grew from 5.24 ms to 33.74 ms.
- Observation construction median per call grew from 4.88 ms to 10.31 ms.
- Prefix copy median per call stayed below 0.31 ms.
- Signal precompute remained one full-frame call and stayed near 53 ms at 500 bars.
- Structure workload counts expanded strongly from 100 to 500 bars: total candidates 470 -> 11903, total zones 427 -> 8375, and total independent zone touches 1302 -> 55083.

Inference: after signal precompute, replay cost on this workload is dominated by repeated point-in-time structure analysis and, secondarily, historical observation construction/fingerprinting. Prefix slicing/copy, signals, state evaluation, and strategy evaluation are clearly not the primary remaining bottlenecks under this benchmark.

## Measurement Limitations

- Segment durations include wrapper overhead from the attribution track.
- Production and instrumented samples are separate runs, so production shares are attribution ratios, not an additive decomposition of one exact run.
- Segment totals are not a claim that every real market workload has the same distribution.
- Structure analysis is measured as one segment; this stage does not split pivot detection, ATR, clustering, and zone observation internally.
- Workload statistics come from returned public snapshots and do not inspect private internals.
- Synthetic prices are deterministic and shaped to exercise structure/signal warm-up, but they are not a market-data representativeness claim.
- Results are local to this machine/runtime and should be used for trend and prioritization, not cross-machine absolute performance promises.

## Go/No-Go Decision

Decision: GO - candidate `v0.44.0 Price Structure Replay Precompute`.

Measured basis for this workload:

- At 500 bars, structure analysis is the largest measured segment: 16.868s, 74.6% of the instrumented median, and 89.2% of the production median as a normalized attribution ratio.
- Structure grows much faster than bars and much faster than prefix copy, state evaluation, strategy evaluation, and signal precompute.
- Observation construction is the second largest cost and may become the next bottleneck after structure optimization.
- The maximum possible production benefit is less than 100%; v0.44.0 cannot eliminate observation construction, prefix copying, state/strategy evaluation, orchestration, or serialization costs.

Risk basis:

- Price structure has strict point-in-time requirements: pivot confirmation, zone availability, future-data isolation, and full-frame position mapping must remain exact.
- The optimization should use the current prefix-based production path as the parity oracle, not copy an optimized implementation as its own reference.
- The GO decision applies to the current deterministic benchmark workload and should be revalidated on any materially different market-data workload.
